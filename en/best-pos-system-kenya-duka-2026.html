What actually matters for a duka, not a features page

Most POS comparisons rank apps by feature count. That's not how a busy shop actually decides. What matters is whether the till still works when KPLC cuts power or your signal drops, whether you can see who owes you money without digging through a notebook, and whether the "free" app is still free once you add the pieces you actually need.

So this comparison scores three things Kenyan owners keep asking about in forums, reviews and Facebook shop-owner groups: deni (customer credit) tracking, working with no internet, and the real monthly cost once add-ons are counted, not the headline "free" price.

Loyverse: the free app most owners already have

Loyverse is probably the POS app you've already tried. It's free for the core till, sales, receipts and basic stock, and Loyverse itself says on its own site that over a million businesses across 170 countries have registered, so it's a genuinely popular starting point, including for general shops in Kenya.

The catch is what sits behind paid add-ons. Employee Management, the module that gives each cashier their own login and role permissions, is listed by pricing-comparison sites at around $25 a month per store as of July 2026, on top of the free core app. If controlling what a junior cashier can discount matters to you, budget for that add-on rather than assuming it's included.

Offline, Loyverse keeps ringing up sales with no connection, according to its own help centre, but some actions, including refunds and adding a new customer, are restricted until you're back online. And for deni specifically: Loyverse users have publicly asked for a native customer-credit feature on the company's own community forum, with some describing workarounds like recording a manual "account balance" payment type. As of July 2026 that's a real gap worth testing yourself rather than assuming it works like a dedicated deni tracker.

Local POS options and the "deni app" gap

Kenya has its own crop of POS vendors built for local shops. SimbaPOS, for example, markets itself as a retail and restaurant POS for Kenya with cash, M-Pesa, card and invoice as accepted payment methods, plus user access rights and shift reports, and it prices as a one-off purchase rather than a monthly subscription, listed by industry pricing directories at roughly $249 one-time as of 2026 (confirm current KES pricing directly, as sites differ and one-off software prices change).

Here's the pattern that stands out once you look closely: a whole separate category of Kenyan apps exists purely to track deni, Duka Digital, Shopcred, Deni App, and Nairobi-built tools covered by local media under headlines like "No deni in my duka." That's a strong signal that a full till plus a genuinely usable customer-credit ledger, in one app, working offline, is still something many Kenyan shop owners can't find in a single free tool, which is exactly the gap worth checking for in whatever you choose.

M-Pesa and eTIMS: what to actually check

M-Pesa reconciliation is the single most common complaint we found in Kenyan POS reviews and forum posts: owners manually matching confirmation SMS messages to sales, which wastes time and invites mistakes. Some POS vendors advertise automatic till reconciliation as a specific paid feature, so if that's your biggest pain point, ask for a live demo of exactly that, not a general "we support M-Pesa" claim.

eTIMS is a separate matter entirely, it's KRA's electronic tax invoicing requirement, and getting it wrong has real penalties. We cover what eTIMS actually requires, OSCU vs VSCU, and the compliance checklist in a dedicated guide, since it deserves its own deep dive rather than a paragraph here. No POS mentioned in this article, including digabloPos, is claimed here as currently eTIMS-certified: confirm current certification status directly with KRA and with any vendor before you rely on it for tax invoicing.

How to test any of these before your first real sale

A features page is marketing. Test the app itself, live, before you trust a real customer's tab or a real day's takings to it.

  • Turn off your phone's data and try to ring up a sale and check a customer's deni balance. If either fails, that's your answer regardless of what the website says.
  • Create two staff logins with different permissions and try to apply a large discount from the lower one. It should be blocked, not just discouraged by a rule you have to remember to enforce.
  • Add a test customer to a tab, record a partial payment, and confirm the balance updates correctly and is visible in one place, not buried inside a general sales report.
  • Ask directly about M-Pesa, cash and any add-on pricing before you commit, in writing if you can, since headline prices rarely include everything you'll actually need.

None of this takes more than twenty minutes, and it's twenty minutes that can save you from migrating your deni list and your stock count off an app that quietly stops doing what you needed three months in.

FAQ

Is Loyverse really free for a duka in Kenya?

The core till, sales, receipts and basic reporting are free with no time limit. But features many shop owners assume are included, like per-employee logins and permissions, sit behind a paid Employee Management add-on that pricing-comparison sites list at around $25 a month per store as of July 2026, so check the current add-on price before assuming your setup stays free.

Does any of these apps reconcile M-Pesa automatically?

Some Kenyan POS vendors advertise automatic M-Pesa till reconciliation as a paid feature, so if that's your main headache, ask specifically for a live demo of it before signing anything. digabloPos does not advertise processing or settling M-Pesa itself: it stays payment-method agnostic, charges no forced commission, and records what you actually collected, cash, M-Pesa or a tab, so you keep the freedom to use your own till number.

Which POS keeps working when there's no internet?

Loyverse can keep selling while offline but restricts some actions, like refunds and adding a new customer, until you're back online, per its own help centre. digabloPos advertises a full offline mode with automatic sync once the connection returns. Whichever app you pick, test it with your phone's data switched off before you trust it on a bad-signal day.

How do I track deni (customers who owe me money) in a POS?

Look for a screen that shows a running balance per customer, not a total buried in your daily sales, and confirm it still works with no signal. digabloPos includes this as customer credit or tabs. Loyverse users have publicly requested a native customer-credit feature on its own community forum, so as of July 2026 it's worth testing directly rather than assuming it works the way a dedicated deni app does.

Is digabloPos eTIMS-compliant or KRA-approved?

We do not claim digabloPos is currently certified or integrated with KRA's eTIMS. It advertises being ready for e-invoicing generally, but eTIMS specifically is a Kenya Revenue Authority requirement with its own approval process, so confirm current eTIMS status directly with digabloPos and see our full eTIMS guide for what KRA actually requires.
